## 1. Scientific Research Foundation

### 1.1 Conspicuous Consumption Theory

Based on Thorstein Veblen's seminal work "The Theory of the Leisure Class" (1899), conspicuous consumption describes the practice of acquiring goods to publicly display economic power and social status. <mcreference link="https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/political-science/veblens-theory-conspicuous-consumption" index="2">2</mcreference> Modern research confirms that "conspicuous consumption is aimed to gain social recognition and signal status" and shows a negative correlation with self-esteem, particularly among high social class individuals. <mcreference link="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7905187/" index="4">4</mcreference>

### 1.2 Status Signaling in Digital Environments

Recent studies demonstrate that "people are more compliant and generous to people who display luxury and are even willing to pay a cost to affiliate with them." <mcreference link="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1090513810001455" index="5">5</mcreference> This validates our core hypothesis that users will pay for digital status symbols even when they provide no functional value.

### 1.3 Economic Inequality and Status Consumption

Research shows that "when economic inequality was high, individuals were more motivated by status consumption" and that "consumption tends to increase when the buying and the using of goods and services is conspicuous." <mcreference link="https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.809101/full" index="1">1</mcreference> This supports our platform's relevance in today's economically stratified crypto ecosystem.
